Indoor Waterfall System

The Indoor Waterfall System is a creative embedded engineering project that simulates a continuous natural water flow using a controlled pump and reservoir system. The design circulates water from a lower tank to an elevated outlet where gravity creates a smooth waterfall effect. The system can be enhanced with lighting effects for aesthetics and relaxation environments, demonstrating principles of fluid dynamics, mechanical design, and basic automation control.

Electromagnetics

Electromagnetic Propulsion Vehicle

Designed and developed an experimental electromagnetic propulsion vehicle that explores the principles of magnetic force interaction and controlled motion. The system uses a combination of permanent magnets and an electromagnet mounted on a track-based vehicle to generate forward thrust through magnetic attraction and repulsion. The project investigates how timed activation of an electromagnet can create directional movement by manipulating magnetic fields along a guided track, simulating linear propulsion concepts used in advanced transport systems. It demonstrates fundamental physics principles such as electromagnetic force, polarity interaction, and energy conversion into mechanical motion.
